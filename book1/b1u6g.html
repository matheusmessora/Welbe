  • The personal pronouns commit the actions and they are before the verb.
  • The possessive adjective pronouns are used to indicate that someone has something and are used before the noun.
  • The possessive pronouns are used to indicate that someone has something and are used after the noun.
  • The objective pronouns receive the actions and they are after the verb.

Examples: That is her car. That car is hers.
Those are my books. Those books are mine.
I need to talk to her. (not I need to talk to she.)

2. MODAL VERBS


These verbs are special becaus: They work like auxiliaries; they have no different conjugation; we never put TO after the modal verb despite of having another verb after.


CAN
It's used to show ability.

Examples: He can do his homework alone.
Can you help me, please? He can not (cannot / can't) go there alone.

COULD
It's used to express conditional possibility or ability and also works as past of CAN or a polite way of asking something.

EXAMPLES:Could you lend me your pen, please?
He could buy a new car if he had money.
He could buy a new car last week.
I could not (couldn't) talk to her, yet.
WOULD
It doesn't have translation and only modifies the verbs for a future condition.

EXAMPLES: They would buy a new car if they had money.
What would you do if you had US$1.000.000 in the bank?
I would not (wouldn't) be here if I had this money in the bank.

NOTICE: When we used the modal verb WOULD with the verb LIKE, it expresses a polite form of asking or offering something.

NOTICE 2: I would = I'd

Examples: What would you like to eat for dinner?
I'd Like to have more free time.
