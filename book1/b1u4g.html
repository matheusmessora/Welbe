1. PRESENT CONTINUOUS OR PROGRESSIVE.


We us the present continuous form when the action is in this moment, or in the future.
It's formed by:
SUBJECT + TO BE + MAIN VERB + ING + COMPLEMENT
Examples: Pete is studying with his girlfriend.
What are you doing on Saturday at 6pm? At 6pm I am working.
They are not eating their food.
NOTICE: We have to pay attention to some terminations of the verbs
TO SMOKE SMOKING
TO SEE SEEING
TO LIE LYING
TO RUN RUNNING
TO PLAY PLAYING
TO SHOW SHOWING
TO FIX FIXING

2. INTERROGATIVE PRONOUNS


WHAT It's about things.
WHERE It's about place.
WHICH It's used when we have options.
HOW It;s about state or condition.
WHEN It's about time.
WHOSE It's used to ask the owner of something.
WHO It's about person.
NOTICE: Special attention when you use WHAT / WHICH / WHO. They have the same translation in some cases, but must be used in different situations.
What: It is used with the same translation of the others in questions and exclamation.

Examples: What time is it? / What a beautiful day!

Which: It is used with the same translation of the others adding information for animals or objects. Example: The company, which Mike works, is moving to Paris.

Who: It is used with the same translation of the others adding information for people. Example: Mike, who works in London, is moving to Paris.
