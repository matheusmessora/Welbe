5. ARTICLES
Definite article Indefinite articles
THE A
AN


Use THE before nouns and never before names. The car is red. The teacher is in the classrom.
Use A before words with sound of consonant. This is a red car. That is a beautiful girl.
Use AN before words with sound of vowel. This is an orange car. That is an ugly girl.
6. PREPOSITIONS

FROM
We use FROM to indicate origin.
Examples: The boys are from Canada. Where are you from?

IN
We use IN to indicate inclusion into a space or periods of time.
Examples:
  • The students are in the classroom.
  • In the morning; in the afternoon; in the evening.
  • In January; in 1987.
  • In the spring; in the summer; in the fall; in the winter.
NOTICE: When we refer to the night, the preposition used is AT; at night.
ON
We use ON when something is in contact with something, before weekdays and specific date.
Examples: The picture is on the wall; My next class is on Monday; On July 7.

8. PLURAL
We normally add S to the noun.
Car / cards;     pen / pens;    computer/computers.

We add ES when the words end by: S / SS / SH / CH / X / O / Z
Bus / buses;      box / boxes;      hero / heroes.
Words ended by consonant + Y: remove Y and add IES.
University / universities;    baby / babies

Words ended by F or FE: remove F or FE and add VES.
Life / lives;     wolf / wolves
NOTICE: Some words do not follow a standard, then just add S.
Example: Dwarf / dwarfs

Some words are never put into the plural.
one information / two information; one fish / two fish; one Japanese / two Japanese;
one aircraft / two aircraft; one hundred / two hundred;

There are some nouns which have irregular plural.
one man / two men; one woman / two women; one child / two children;
one tooth / two teeth; one person / two people or two persons (formal);
one foot / two feet;
