Grammar - Different lives

1. FREQUENCY ADVERBS


The planned future is used to speak about something that will happen in the future. The rule is:


100% ALWAYS
80% USUALLY
70% OFTEN
50% SOMETIMES/OCCASIONALLY
20% SELDOM
10% RARELY
0% NEVER

We use the frequency adverbs to know the frequency something happens or the frequency someone does something.
To make the question we use: How often and most of the time the questions are made in the Simple Present. Examples: How often do you go to the cinema? I rarely go to the cinema.
How often does she dance? She usually dances.
How often can you travel alone? I can never travel alone.
How often are you at home? I'm always at home

NOTICE: Take care because there is an order to put the adverbs and you have to respect this order. The order is: Subject + adverb + main verb.

Howerver, when you use SOMETIMES or OCCASIONALLY you can start the sentences and when you have TO BE or MODAL VERBS, you never separate them of the subject.

Examples: Sometimes I do my lessons at night. = I sometimes do my lessons at night.
I am always happy on Fridays.
I can always help you if you ask me.
