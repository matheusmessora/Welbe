GRAMMAR - IN THE LIBRARY

1. VERB THERE TO BE


  • When we see the adverb THERE, we have to imagine opposed to HERE.

  • When we see the verb THERE TO BE conjugated in the present we see THERE IS in the singular, or THERE ARE in the plural. They are used to say that exists something somewhere.

Examples:
Gabi's doll is over there.
There is a good film on TV tonight.
Are there many people in the house?
What is there in the refrigerator?
There isn't any book inside the drawer.

2. INTERROGATIVE PRONOUNS


WHAT It's about things.
WHERE It's about place.
WHICH It's used when we have options.
WHEN It's about time.
HOW It;s about state or condition.
WHOSE It's used to ask the owner of something.

Examples: Whose book is this? / Henry, whose father is a businessman, is on the beach.

3- PRONOUNS


ONE
It's used to substitute a noun said previously and said in the singular.
Example: The red car is more beautiful than the black one. (black car)

ONES
It's used to substitute a noun said previously and said in the plural.
Example: I prefer the blue pens to the black ons. (black pens)
