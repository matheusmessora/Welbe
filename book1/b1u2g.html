1. PRONOUNS

The personal pronouns commit the actions and they are before the verb.
The possessive adjective pronouns are used to indicate that someone has something and are used before the noun.
The possessive pronouns are used to indicate that someone has something and are used after the noun.

Examples: He is not her brother.
That book is hers and the dictionary is mine.
A friend of mine is in Los Angeles with his family.

2. GENITIVE CASE


It's used to show possessions or close conection between two things.


The book of Mike is red.                    = Mike's book is red.
The book of Mike and Tom is red.    = Mike and Tom's book is red.
The book of Marcos is old.                = Marcos's (s) book is old.
The book of the child is new.             = The child's book is new.

4. PREPOSITIONS

OF
It's used to indicate that something belongs to someone.

EXAMPLES: The doll of the girl is very beautiful.
THe love of a mother for a child is something incredible.

AT
It's used before: specific time; commemorative date; specific place.

Examples: My next class is on Monday at 3pm.
The meeting is at Christmas.
The meeting is at Peter's restaurant.

AMONG = in the middle of 3 or more things. INSIDE = in a closed or delimited place.
BEHING = at the back of something. NEAR = close to something.
BESIDE = at one of the sides of something. UNDER = opposed to ON.
BETWEEN = in the middle of 2 things. WITH = accompanied by.

5. INTERROGATIVE PRONOUNS

WHAT: It's about things.
WHERE: It's about place.
WHICH: It's used when we have options
WHEN: It's used when we have options
HOW: It's about state or condition.

Examples: What is her name? Her name is Ann.
Where is your schoold? It's in SP.
Which book is yours? The blue book is mine
When is his birthday? His birthday is in July.
How is she? She is fine.
