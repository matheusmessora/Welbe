1. SIMPLE PRESENT.

We use the simple present form when want to refer about routines. It's formed by:


SUBJECT + MAIN VERD + COMPLEMENT (affirmative)

I
YOU WORK IN SP.
WE WATCH TV EVERY NIGHT.
YOU STUDY ENGLISH AT WELBE PRIVATE CLASS.
THEY


But when we refer to HE / SHE / IT the rule is:
SUBJECT + MAIN VERD + S (ES / IES) + COMPLEMENT (affirmative)
We normally add S to the verd, but verbs ended by: S / SS / SH / CH / X / O / Z we add
ES and verbs ended by: consonant + Y we change to consonant + IES.


HE WORKS IN SP.
SHE WATCHES TV EVERY NIGHT.
IT STUDIES ENGLISH AT WELBE PRIVATE CLASS

Notice: Attention with the verb TO HAVE. I have a car. He has a car.
And not: He haves a car.


In interrogative the rule is: DO + SUBJECT + MAINVERB + COMPLEMENT


I
YOU WORK IN SP?
DO WE WATCH TV EVERY NIGHT?
YOU STUDY ENGLISH AT WELBE PRIVATE CLASS?
THEY


And for HE / SHE / IT the rule is: DOES + SUBJECT + MAIN VERD + COMPLEMENT


HE WORK IN SP?
DOES SHE WATCH TV EVERY NIGHT?
IT STUDY ENGLISH AT WELBE PRIVATE CLASS?

In negative: SUBJECT + DO + NOT (DON'T) + MAIN VERD + COMPLEMENT


I
YOU WORK IN SP.
WE DON'T WATCH TV EVERY NIGHT.
YOU STUDY ENGLISH AT WELBE PRIVATE CLASS.


And for HE / SHE / IT: SUBJECT + DOES + NO (DOESN'T) + MAIN VERB + COMPLEMENT


HE WORK IN SP.
SHE DOESN'T WATCH TV EVERY NIGHT.
IT STUDY ENGLISH AT WELBE PRIVATE CLASS.

NOTICE: Pay attentation to the verb when the auxiliary DOES is used, because the verb
needs to lose the S, ES or IES.
